# Matching Rubric / 画像与业务匹配度

## Evidence levels

- 已确认: explicitly stated on website/public source.
- 合理推测: inferred from product/category; must use cautious language.
- 待确认: needs human/customer confirmation.

## 航线匹配

High:
- Source mentions Middle East, Saudi, UAE, Egypt, Jordan, Red Sea, India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, US, Europe, Africa.
- Site has overseas distributor/project pages in those regions.

Medium:
- Site says global/export/OEM/ODM but no region.
- Product category commonly exports by sea, but market is unclear.

Low/unknown:
- Domestic-only signals or no export/global information.

Never infer a specific port unless source states it. Use regions like “中东方向可能相关，目的港待确认”.

## 货类匹配

Strong sea-freight fit:
- home appliances, lighting, furniture, home goods, building materials, ceramics, sanitary ware, hardware, auto parts, machinery, daily goods.

Conditional fit:
- electronics, batteries, new energy parts, high-value goods: may require compliance, insurance, air/sea comparison, MSDS/DG checks.

Weak fit:
- pure software, local services, consulting, content, restaurants, domestic retail-only.

## 出货规模匹配

High potential, but not exact volume:
- factory/manufacturer, OEM/ODM, global distributors, large product catalog, project cases, export certifications, multiple overseas sites.

Medium:
- trading company, smaller catalog, some export signals.

Unknown:
- no logistics/export evidence.

Never write “每月 X 柜” unless public source explicitly states it.

## Overall matching

A:
- Clear export/global signal + cargo category fits sea freight + likely route or market aligns with our routes.

B:
- Category fits but route/scale unclear; good for discovery outreach.

C:
- Sparse information or unclear fit; needs enrichment.

Reject/archive:
- Clearly not a freight customer or outside our business.
